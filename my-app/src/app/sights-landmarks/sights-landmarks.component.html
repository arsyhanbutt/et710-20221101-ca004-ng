Landmarks and Historical Buildings near Ozone Park

Rigewood Reservior

A 2.3 mile lightly trafficked loop trail, located near Ozone Park, New York City, NY. It features a lake. The trail is suitable for all activities such as dog-walking, although primarily used for running and walking.

Vistors have described it as clean, peaceful, and great for biking. Many have reported being able to use the trail because it supported their various skill levels of walking and running.

Location:
West Of Cypress Hills
St/South Of Jackie
Robinson Pkwy
New York, NY 11385

Queens Borough Hall

A historical and public building in the Kew Gardens neighborhood (near Ozone Park). It was designed by architechs William Gehron and Andrew J. Thomas and was completed in 1940. It was designed to serve as the center of civic life and now has become a popular spot for marriages.

Vistors have described the people and staff as helpful and effecient.

Location:
120-55 Queens Blvd
Kew Gardens
NY 11415

Van Wyck Expressway

A road highway that connects John F. Kennedy Airport and the Whitestone Expressway and the Brox Whitestone Bridge further north. It was constructed between 1947 and 1963 and it was named after the first mayor of the unified New York City, Robert C. Van Wyck.

Being one of the not so popular areas to visit, visitors have described this place as slow during non-peak hours.

Location:
105-32 Van Wyck Expy
Queens
NY 11419
